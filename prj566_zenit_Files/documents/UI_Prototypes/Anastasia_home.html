   About program:

In PRJ566 students work in groups of 3 or 4 to carry out a computer system development project. Analysis and design for the project take place in PRJ566 while coding, testing and implementation take place in PRJ666. The project might be a computer system for a real client, an open source system, a game, or some kind of interactive educational tool or resource. Students also learn project management fundamentals, including planning, scheduling, risk evaluation, and cost benefit analysis. Tools that the students will use include Rational Rose for software modeling. Common PRJ566 elements such as assignments, tutorials, course documents, and course policies are accessible from this main site. Also check the week by week course schedule and detailed notes available on the main PRJ566 site and on instructor Web sites.
